# Yosemite Campground Elevation and Climate Analysis

## Climate Background (from Wikipedia)
Yosemite has a Mediterranean climate (Köppen classification Csa), meaning most precipitation falls during the mild winter, and other seasons are nearly dry. Less than three percent of precipitation falls during the long, hot summers. Precipitation increases with elevation up to 8,000 feet (2,400 m) then slowly decreases to the crest. Precipitation amounts vary from 36 inches (910 mm) at 4,000 ft elevation to 50 inches (1,300 mm) at 8,600 ft.
